# Fertilizer & Soil Health Guide — Knowledge Base for AgroBuddy
# Source: ICAR, TNAU, Indian Fertilizer Association

## Soil Testing

### Importance
- Get soil tested every 2-3 years from nearest Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK)
- Soil test helps determine exact nutrient requirements
- Saves money by avoiding unnecessary fertilizer application
- Government-subsidized testing available at ₹10-50 per sample

### How to Collect Soil Sample
1. Collect samples from 8-10 spots in the field in zigzag pattern
2. Remove surface debris and dig 15cm (6 inches) deep
3. Take a slice of soil from the side of the hole
4. Mix all samples together in a clean bucket
5. Take about 500g of the mixed sample
6. Dry in shade (not in sun)
7. Label with name, field location, and crop to be grown
8. Submit to nearest soil testing lab

### Key Soil Parameters
- **pH**: Ideal range 6.0-7.5 for most crops
  - Acidic soil (pH <6): Apply lime (2-4 tonnes/ha)
  - Alkaline soil (pH >8): Apply gypsum (2-5 tonnes/ha)
- **Organic Carbon**: Should be >0.5% (ideally >0.75%)
- **Available Nitrogen (N)**: Low < 280 kg/ha, Medium 280-560, High >560
- **Available Phosphorus (P)**: Low < 10 kg/ha, Medium 10-25, High >25
- **Available Potassium (K)**: Low < 108 kg/ha, Medium 108-280, High >280
- **Micronutrients**: Zinc, Iron, Manganese, Copper, Boron

## Organic Fertilizers

### Farmyard Manure (FYM)
- **What**: Decomposed mixture of cattle dung, urine, and bedding material
- **NPK Content**: 0.5-1.0% N, 0.15-0.30% P, 0.5-1.0% K
- **Application**: 5-10 tonnes per acre, apply 2-3 weeks before sowing
- **Benefits**: Improves soil structure, water-holding capacity, and microbial activity
- **Cost**: ₹500-1500 per tonne (local sourcing cheapest)

### Vermicompost
- **What**: Compost produced by earthworms (Eisenia fetida)
- **NPK Content**: 1.5-2.0% N, 0.9-1.7% P, 1.5-2.4% K
- **Application**: 2-5 tonnes per acre
- **Benefits**: Excellent for all crops, improves soil biology, disease resistance
- **Home Production**: Can be made from kitchen waste and cow dung in 45-60 days
- **Cost**: ₹5-10 per kg (cheaper if made on-farm)

### Green Manure
- **Best Crops**: Dhaincha (Sesbania), Sun hemp (Crotalaria), Moong/Urd beans
- **How**: Grow for 45-60 days and plow under before flowering
- **NPK Benefit**: Adds 60-80 kg nitrogen per hectare
- **Best Time**: Before kharif/monsoon season (July sowing)
- **Additional Benefit**: Suppresses weeds, improves soil organic matter

### Compost
- **Preparation**: Layer crop residues, farm waste, and cow dung. Turn every 15 days. Ready in 3-4 months.
- **Application**: 5-8 tonnes per acre

### Neem Cake
- **NPK Content**: 5.2% N, 1.0% P, 1.4% K
- **Application**: 100-200 kg per acre mixed in soil at sowing
- **Benefits**: Also acts as bio-pesticide, controls nematodes
- **Cost**: ₹12-20 per kg

### Bio-fertilizers
- **Rhizobium**: For legume crops (pulses, groundnut). 200g per 10 kg seed.
- **Azotobacter**: For non-legume crops. Fixes atmospheric nitrogen. 200g/10 kg seed.
- **Azospirillum**: For cereals and millets.
- **Phosphobacteria (PSB)**: Solubilizes fixed phosphorus. 200g/10 kg seed.
- **Mycorrhiza (VAM)**: Increases phosphorus uptake. Apply in nursery.
- **Application**: Seed treatment or soil application. Keep away from chemical fertilizers.

## Chemical Fertilizers

### Major Fertilizers (NPK)

#### Urea (46% N)
- **Use**: Nitrogen source for all crops
- **Application**: Split into 2-3 doses. Basal + top dressing at tillering + panicle initiation.
- **Rate**: Varies by crop (Rice: 100-120 kg N/ha = 220-260 kg urea/ha)
- **Tip**: Apply before irrigation or light rain for best absorption
- **Cost**: ₹267 per 45 kg bag (subsidized)
- **Warning**: Never over-apply — excess nitrogen makes crops prone to disease and lodging

#### DAP (18% N, 46% P)
- **Use**: Phosphorus source + starter nitrogen
- **Application**: At sowing/transplanting only (phosphorus doesn't move in soil)
- **Rate**: 100-150 kg/ha for most crops
- **Cost**: ₹1350 per 50 kg bag (subsidized)

#### MOP / Muriate of Potash (60% K)
- **Use**: Potassium source, strengthens plant structure and disease resistance
- **Application**: Basal dose at sowing + top dressing in some crops
- **Rate**: 50-80 kg/ha
- **Cost**: ₹1700 per 50 kg bag

#### SSP / Single Super Phosphate (16% P, 11% S)
- **Use**: Phosphorus + Sulphur source
- **Application**: Basal dose at sowing
- **Advantage**: Cheaper than DAP, also provides sulphur
- **Rate**: 200-300 kg/ha

#### NPK Complex (various grades)
- Common grades: 10:26:26, 12:32:16, 20:20:0
- **Use**: Balanced nutrition at sowing
- **Application**: Apply in furrows at sowing time

### Micronutrient Fertilizers

#### Zinc Sulphate (33% Zn)
- **Deficiency Signs**: Stunted growth, whitish bands along midrib in rice (khaira disease)
- **Soil Application**: 25 kg/ha at sowing
- **Foliar Spray**: 0.5% ZnSO4 (5g/liter) + 0.25% lime
- **Critical for**: Rice, wheat, maize, citrus

#### Ferrous Sulphate (19% Fe)
- **Deficiency Signs**: Interveinal chlorosis (yellowing between veins) of young leaves
- **Foliar Spray**: 0.5-1% FeSO4 solution
- **Common in**: Alkaline/calcareous soils

#### Borax (11% B)
- **Deficiency Signs**: Hollow heart in cauliflower, flowers fall without fruit in mustard
- **Application**: 10 kg/ha soil application or 0.2% spray
- **Critical for**: Cauliflower, mustard, sunflower

## Crop-Specific Fertilizer Recommendations (per acre)

### Rice (Paddy)
- FYM/Compost: 5 tonnes
- N:P:K = 50:25:25 kg/acre (= 110 kg Urea + 55 kg DAP + 42 kg MOP)
- Zinc Sulphate: 10 kg/acre in zinc-deficient soils
- Timing: Basal (50% P, K, 25% N) → 25% N at tillering → 25% N at panicle initiation

### Wheat
- FYM: 5 tonnes
- N:P:K = 60:25:15 kg/acre
- Timing: Half N + full P + full K at sowing → Half N at first irrigation (21 DAS)

### Tomato
- FYM: 10 tonnes
- N:P:K = 50:25:25 kg/acre
- Micronutrients: Borax 4 kg/acre
- Timing: 50% N + full P + 50% K basal → 25% N at flowering → 25% N + 50% K at fruiting

### Potato
- FYM: 10 tonnes
- N:P:K = 60:30:40 kg/acre
- High potassium requirement for tuber quality

### Cotton
- FYM: 5 tonnes
- N:P:K = 30:15:15 kg/acre
- Apply N in 3 splits: sowing, square formation, boll development

### Sugarcane
- FYM: 10 tonnes
- N:P:K = 100:30:50 kg/acre
- Heavy feeder — needs multiple N top dressings

## Best Practices

### Application Tips
1. Always apply fertilizer based on soil test results
2. Never mix urea with SSP — it causes nitrogen loss
3. Apply fertilizer in moist soil, not dry soil
4. Cover fertilizer with soil to reduce volatilization loss
5. Don't apply near plant stem — keep 5-8cm distance
6. Band placement is more efficient than broadcasting
7. Use neem-coated urea for slow release (mandatory in India since 2015)
8. Split nitrogen application always gives better results

### Safety Precautions
- Store fertilizers in dry place away from children and animals
- Never eat or drink while handling fertilizers
- Wash hands thoroughly after application
- Don't apply chemical fertilizers and bio-fertilizers together at same time
- Keep chemical and organic fertilizers separate during storage
