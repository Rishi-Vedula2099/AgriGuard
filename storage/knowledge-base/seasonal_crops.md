| Jute | March-May | Aug-Sep | WB, Bihar, Assam |

### Weather Considerations
- **Pre-monsoon (May-June)**: Prepare land, apply FYM, plan seed procurement
- **Monsoon onset**: Sow after 2-3 good rains (soil has sufficient moisture)
- **Mid-monsoon (Aug)**: Critical pest/disease monitoring period
- **Late monsoon (Sep-Oct)**: Harvest planning, post-harvest processing
- **Cyclone risk**: Coastal areas — harvest early if cyclone warning

## Rabi Season (Winter: October-March)

### Major Crops
| Crop | Sowing | Harvest | Key States |
|------|--------|---------|------------|
| Wheat | Nov-Dec | Mar-Apr | Punjab, Haryana, UP, MP |
| Mustard | Oct-Nov | Feb-Mar | Rajasthan, MP, UP |
| Chickpea (Chana) | Oct-Nov | Feb-Mar | MP, Rajasthan, Maharashtra |
| Barley | Nov-Dec | Mar-Apr | Rajasthan, UP, MP |
| Peas | Oct-Nov | Jan-Feb | UP, MP, Punjab |
| Lentil (Masoor) | Oct-Nov | Feb-Mar | MP, UP, Bihar |
| Potato | Oct-Nov | Jan-Feb | UP, WB, Punjab |
| Onion | Oct-Nov (kharif), Dec-Jan (rabi) | Varies | Maharashtra, MP, Karnataka |
| Sunflower | Dec-Jan | Mar-Apr | Karnataka, AP, Maharashtra |
| Linseed | Oct-Nov | Feb-Mar | MP, UP, Chhattisgarh |

### Weather Considerations
- **Timely sowing critical**: Late sowing reduces wheat yield by 25-30 kg/ha per day delay after Nov 25
- **Frost protection**: Cover nurseries with polythene. Apply light irrigation. Smoke/heat near field edges.
- **Fog**: Increases disease pressure (rust in wheat, powdery mildew). Be ready with fungicide.
- **Western disturbances**: Unpredictable rain — useful for rainfed crops but may delay harvest

## Crop Rotation Recommendations

### Best Rotations (Disease Control + Soil Health)
1. **Rice → Wheat** (most common in Indo-Gangetic plains)
2. **Rice → Mustard** (good for soil structure)
3. **Cotton → Wheat** (breaks disease cycle)
4. **Soybean → Wheat** (nitrogen fixation benefits wheat)
5. **Maize → Chickpea** (cereal → legume rotation)
6. **Tomato → Rice** (breaks Phytophthora cycle)
7. **Potato → Maize → Wheat** (3-year rotation)

### Rules
- Always rotate cereals with legumes (pulses add nitrogen)
- Don't repeat same crop family (solanaceae: tomato, potato, brinjal, chilli)
- Include a green manure crop every 2-3 years
- Deep-rooted crop after shallow-rooted (rice → mustard)

## Pest & Disease Calendar

### January-February
- Wheat: Watch for rust, aphids
- Mustard: Aphid peak season, apply dimethoate if severe
- Potato: Late blight risk in foggy weather

### March-April
- Wheat: Terminal heat — irrigate at grain filling
- Mango: Powdery mildew on flowers — spray sulphur
- Summer vegetables: Start nurseries

### May-June
- Field preparation for kharif
- Deep ploughing to kill soil pests
- Apply FYM/compost 3-4 weeks before sowing

### July-August
- Rice: Blast, BLB, stem borer — regular monitoring
- Cotton: Bollworm watch — use pheromone traps
- Soybean: Girdle beetle, semi-looper

### September-October
- Rice: Brown plant hopper — avoid excess nitrogen
- Cotton: Pink bollworm — pick affected bolls
- Kharif harvest preparation

### November-December
- Wheat sowing — don't delay beyond Nov 25
- Chickpea: Helicoverpa pod borer — monitor at flowering
- Rabi pest establishment period

## Government Schemes for Farmers

### Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)
- ₹6,000/year in 3 installments to all small and marginal farmers
- Direct bank transfer

### Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)
- Crop insurance at 2% premium for kharif, 1.5% for rabi
- Full loss coverage. Apply before season deadline at bank/CSC.

### PM Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)
- Subsidy for drip/sprinkler irrigation (55-80%)
- Farm pond subsidies
- Apply at District Agriculture Office

### Soil Health Card Scheme
- Free soil testing every 2 years
- Personalized fertilizer recommendations
- Available at KVK or through Soil Health Card portal

### KCC (Kisan Credit Card)
- Crop loans at 4% interest (with prompt repayment benefit)
- Apply at any bank branch

## Climate-Smart Practices

### For Increasing Temperature
- Choose heat-tolerant varieties
- Mulching to reduce soil temperature
- Pre-dawn irrigation
- Shade nets for nurseries and sensitive crops

### For Erratic Rainfall
- Rainwater harvesting (farm ponds)
- Short-duration varieties (escape drought)
- Drought-tolerant varieties (DRR Dhan 44 for rice)
- Crop insurance enrollment

### For Floods
- Submergence-tolerant rice varieties (Swarna Sub-1)
- Raised bed planting
- Drainage channel preparation before monsoon
