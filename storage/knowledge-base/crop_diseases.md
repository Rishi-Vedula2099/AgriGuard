## Tomato Diseases

### Tomato Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)
- **Symptoms**: Dark brown to black water-soaked spots on leaves, stems, and fruits. White fuzzy growth on leaf undersides in humid conditions. Rapid browning and wilting of entire plants.
- **Favorable Conditions**: Cool temperatures (15-22°C), high humidity (>90%), prolonged leaf wetness.
- **Spread**: Wind-blown sporangia can travel several kilometers. Rain splash spreads spores. Survives in infected tubers and plant debris.
- **Organic Treatment**:
  - Bordeaux mixture (1% solution): Mix 100g copper sulphate + 100g lime in 10 liters water. Spray every 7-10 days.
  - Neem oil spray: 5ml neem oil per liter water. Spray in evening.
  - Trichoderma viride: Apply 5g per liter water as soil drench.
  - Remove and burn all infected plant parts immediately.
- **Chemical Treatment**:
  - Mancozeb 75% WP: 2.5g per liter water. Spray every 10-12 days.
  - Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold): 2g per liter water for severe cases.
  - Chlorothalonil 75% WP: 2g per liter water.
  - Safety: Wear gloves, mask, and full-sleeve clothing while spraying. Wait 7 days before harvest.
- **Prevention**:
  - Use resistant varieties (Arka Rakshak, Arka Samrat).
  - Proper spacing: 60cm between plants, 90cm between rows.
  - Avoid overhead irrigation; use drip irrigation.
  - Stake plants to improve air circulation.
  - Crop rotation: Don't plant tomato/potato in same field for 2 years.
  - Remove volunteers and weed hosts.
- **Economic Impact**: Can cause 50-100% crop loss if untreated.

### Tomato Early Blight (Alternaria solani)
- **Symptoms**: Dark brown concentric rings (bull's-eye pattern) on older leaves first. Yellowing around spots. Premature defoliation starting from bottom of plant. Can affect stems and fruits.
- **Favorable Conditions**: Warm temperatures (24-29°C), alternating wet and dry periods.
- **Organic Treatment**:
  - Neem oil: 3-5ml per liter water, spray weekly.
  - Baking soda spray: 1 tablespoon baking soda + few drops dish soap in 1 liter water.
  - Mulch around base to prevent soil splash.
  - Trichoderma harzianum: 5g per liter water.
- **Chemical Treatment**:
  - Chlorothalonil 75% WP: 2g per liter water.
  - Mancozeb 75% WP: 2.5g per liter water.
  - Safety: Follow label instructions strictly.
- **Prevention**:
  - Remove lower leaves touching soil.
  - Stake and prune for air flow.
  - Clean up plant debris after harvest.
  - Use disease-free seeds/seedlings.

### Tomato Bacterial Spot (Xanthomonas)
- **Symptoms**: Small, dark, water-soaked spots on leaves (2-3mm). Yellow halos around spots. Raised, scabby spots on fruits. Leaf edges may turn brown.
- **Organic Treatment**:
  - Copper hydroxide spray (2g per liter).
  - Remove severely infected plants.
  - Avoid working with plants when wet.
- **Chemical Treatment**:
  - Streptomycin sulfate: 0.5g per liter water (only in severe cases).
  - Copper oxychloride 50% WP: 3g per liter.
- **Prevention**:
  - Use certified disease-free seeds.
  - Hot water seed treatment: Soak seeds at 50°C for 25 minutes.
  - Practice 2-3 year crop rotation.

### Tomato Leaf Mold (Passalora fulva)
- **Symptoms**: Yellow spots on upper leaf surface. Olive-green to brown velvety growth on undersides. Severe cases cause leaf curling and drop.
- **Favorable Conditions**: High humidity >85%, temperatures 22-26°C.
- **Treatment**: Improve ventilation. Remove affected leaves. Apply copper-based fungicide. Use resistant varieties.

### Tomato Septoria Leaf Spot (Septoria lycopersici)
- **Symptoms**: Many small circular spots (2-5mm) with dark borders and gray centers. Tiny black specks (pycnidia) in centers. Starts on lower leaves.
- **Treatment**: Remove lower affected leaves. Apply Mancozeb or Chlorothalonil. Avoid overhead watering. Mulch to prevent splash.

### Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV)
- **Symptoms**: Severe upward curling of leaves. Yellowing of leaf margins. Stunted growth. Reduced fruit set.
- **Spread**: Transmitted by whiteflies (Bemisia tabaci).
- **Treatment**: No cure once infected. Control whiteflies with yellow sticky traps, neem oil spray, or imidacloprid (chemical). Remove infected plants. Use resistant varieties (Arka Ananya).

### Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV)
- **Symptoms**: Mottled light and dark green patches on leaves. Leaf distortion and curling. Stunted growth. Fruit may show internal browning.
- **Spread**: Mechanically through contaminated hands, tools, and seeds.
- **Treatment**: No cure. Remove infected plants. Wash hands with soap and milk solution. Disinfect tools with 10% bleach. Use virus-free certified seeds.

## Rice Diseases

### Rice Blast (Magnaporthe oryzae)
- **Symptoms**: Diamond-shaped lesions with gray centers and brown borders on leaves. Neck blast affects panicle base causing white/empty grains. Node blast causes stem breakage.
- **Favorable Conditions**: Temperature 25-28°C, high humidity, heavy nitrogen fertilization, extended dew periods.
- **Organic Treatment**:
  - Pseudomonas fluorescens seed treatment: 10g per kg seed.
  - Trichoderma viride: 4g per kg seed.
  - Silicon-based fertilizer to strengthen cell walls.
- **Chemical Treatment**:
  - Tricyclazole 75% WP: 0.6g per liter water. Apply at tillering and before heading.
  - Isoprothiolane 40% EC: 1.5ml per liter water.
  - Carbendazim 50% WP: 1g per liter water.
  - Safety: PHI (Pre-Harvest Interval) of 15 days.
- **Prevention**:
  - Use blast-resistant varieties (CO 51, ADT 43, IR 64).
  - Balanced nitrogen: Don't exceed recommended dose (usually 120 kg N/ha).
  - Seed treatment with carbendazim 2g/kg seed.
  - Avoid continuous flooding.
  - Remove and destroy stubble after harvest.
- **Economic Impact**: Yield loss 10-30% in endemic areas, up to 100% in severe epidemics.

### Rice Brown Spot (Bipolaris oryzae / Helminthosporium oryzae)
- **Symptoms**: Oval brown spots (1-14mm) on leaves with gray centers. Spots may merge. Seed discoloration. Can affect seedlings (seedling blight).
- **Favorable Conditions**: Nutrient-deficient soils (especially low potassium and zinc), temperatures 25-30°C.
- **Organic Treatment**:
  - Pseudomonas fluorescens seed treatment.
  - Apply zinc sulfate 25kg/ha to correct deficiency.
  - Neem cake application: 150kg/ha.
- **Chemical Treatment**:
  - Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g per liter water.
  - Edifenphos 50% EC: 1ml per liter water.
  - Propiconazole 25% EC: 1ml per liter water.
- **Prevention**:
  - Soil test and correct nutrient deficiencies.
  - Use healthy, certified seeds.
  - Balanced fertilization including micronutrients.
  - Remove volunteer rice plants.

### Rice Sheath Blight (Rhizoctonia solani)
- **Symptoms**: Oval/irregular greenish-gray water-soaked lesions on leaf sheaths near water line. Lesions enlarge and merge. White-gray fungal growth. Can cause lodging.
- **Favorable Conditions**: Dense planting, excessive nitrogen, warm temperatures (28-32°C), continuous flooding.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Hexaconazole 5% SC: 2ml per liter water. Carbendazim 50% WP: 1g/liter. Validamycin 3% SL: 2ml/liter.
- **Prevention**: Optimum spacing (20x15cm). Avoid excess nitrogen. Drain standing water periodically.

### Bacterial Leaf Blight of Rice (Xanthomonas oryzae)
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked yellow lesions starting from leaf tips or edges. Lesions turn straw-colored. Milky bacterial ooze on leaves in morning. Kresek (seedling wilt) in severe cases.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Streptomycin sulphate + Tetracycline (300ppm). Copper oxychloride 50% WP.
- **Prevention**: Use resistant varieties (Improved Samba Mahsuri). Balanced fertilizer. Avoid clipping during transplanting.

## Wheat Diseases

### Wheat Brown/Leaf Rust (Puccinia triticina)
- **Symptoms**: Small, round, orange-brown pustules randomly distributed on leaf surfaces. Pustules release powdery spores when touched.
- **Favorable Conditions**: Moderate temperatures (15-22°C), dew on leaves.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Propiconazole 25% EC: 1ml per liter water at flag leaf stage. Tebuconazole 25.9% EC: 1ml/liter.
- **Prevention**: Use rust-resistant varieties (HD 2967, HD 3086). Timely sowing (November). Avoid late nitrogen application.

### Wheat Yellow/Stripe Rust (Puccinia striiformis)
- **Symptoms**: Yellow-orange pustules arranged in stripes along leaf veins. More severe in cooler weather.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Same as brown rust. Apply at first symptom appearance.
- **Prevention**: Early sowing. Resistant varieties. Remove volunteer wheat.

### Wheat Powdery Mildew (Blumeria graminis)
- **Symptoms**: White powdery patches on upper leaf surfaces. Later turns gray. Reduces photosynthesis.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Sulphur 80% WP: 3g/liter. Propiconazole at flag leaf stage.

### Karnal Bunt (Tilletia indica)
- **Symptoms**: Partial conversion of grain to dark-colored, foul-smelling (fishy) spore mass. Affects a few grains per ear.
- **Treatment**: Seed treatment with Carbendazim + Mancozeb. Avoid late sowing.

## Potato Diseases

### Potato Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)
- **Symptoms**: Same pathogen as tomato late blight. Dark brown water-soaked lesions on leaves. White mold on undersides. Tuber rot (reddish-brown granular rot).
- **Chemical Treatment**: Mancozeb 75% WP: 2.5g/liter preventive spray. Cymoxanil + Mancozeb for curative action. Spray every 7-10 days during favorable conditions.
- **Prevention**: Use certified disease-free seed potatoes. Hill up properly. Destroy cull piles. Harvest in dry weather.

### Potato Early Blight (Alternaria solani)
- **Symptoms**: Same as tomato early blight. Brown concentric ring spots on lower leaves first.
- **Treatment**: Same chemical options as tomato. Maintain adequate nutrition. Avoid plant stress.

## Cotton Diseases

### Cotton Bacterial Blight (Xanthomonas citri pv. malvacearum)
- **Symptoms**: Angular water-soaked spots on leaves. Black arm on stems. Boll rot.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Streptomycin 100ppm + Copper oxychloride 2.5g/liter.
- **Prevention**: Use resistant varieties (Bunny, Brahma). Acid-delinting of seeds. Crop rotation.

### Cotton Fusarium Wilt (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. vasinfectum)
- **Symptoms**: Progressive wilting starting from lower leaves. Yellowing. Brown discoloration of vascular tissue.
- **Treatment**: Soil application of Trichoderma viride 2.5 kg/ha. Carbendazim soil drench for severe cases. Use resistant varieties.

## Sugarcane Diseases

### Sugarcane Red Rot (Colletotrichum falcatum)
- **Symptoms**: Reddening of internal stalk tissue with white transverse bands. Drying of crown. Withering of inner leaves.
- **Treatment**: Use resistant varieties. Hot water treatment of setts (50°C for 2 hours). Carbendazim sett treatment.

### Sugarcane Smut (Sporisorium scitamineum)
- **Symptoms**: Black whip-like structure emerging from growing point. Stunted growth.
- **Treatment**: Resistant varieties only effective control. Remove and burn infected plants. Hot water treat setts.

## Soybean Diseases

### Soybean Rust (Phakopsora pachyrhizi)
- **Symptoms**: Tan to brown lesions on lower leaves. Pustules on undersides. Premature defoliation.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Propiconazole or Tebuconazole at R1-R3 stage.
- **Prevention**: Early planting. Resistant varieties. Monitor regularly.

## Groundnut Diseases

### Tikka Disease / Leaf Spot (Cercospora)
- **Symptoms**: Dark brown circular spots on leaves. Severe defoliation reduces pod filling.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Mancozeb 2.5g/liter at 30, 45, and 60 DAS.
- **Prevention**: Use resistant varieties. Crop rotation with cereals.

## Grape Diseases

### Grape Downy Mildew (Plasmopara viticola)
- **Symptoms**: Yellow spots on upper leaf surface. White cottony growth on undersides. Fruit infection causes shrinking.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Mancozeb preventive spray. Metalaxyl + Mancozeb for curative. Copper-based sprays.

### Grape Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe necator)
- **Symptoms**: White powdery growth on all green parts. Berry cracking.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Sulphur 80% WP. Penconazole 10% EC. Avoid spraying sulphur above 35°C.

## Mango Diseases

### Mango Anthracnose (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides)
- **Symptoms**: Black spots on flowers, leaves, and fruits. Fruit rot during transit.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Carbendazim 1g/liter spray at flowering. Pre-harvest hot water treatment of fruits.

### Mango Powdery Mildew (Oidium mangiferae)
- **Symptoms**: White powdery coating on inflorescence. Flower and fruitlet drop.
- **Chemical Treatment**: Sulphur 80% WP at panicle emergence. Repeat at 15-day intervals.
