# Yield Improvement Strategies — Knowledge Base for AgroBuddy
# Source: ICAR, Agricultural Research Stations, Farmer Best Practices

## Top 10 Yield Killers in Indian Farming

1. **Poor seed quality** — 15-20% yield reduction
2. **Incorrect spacing** — 10-20% loss from overcrowding or wide spacing
3. **Imbalanced fertilization** — 15-25% loss from nitrogen-only approach
4. **Late sowing** — 20-30% loss (especially wheat, mustard)
5. **Water stress at critical stages** — 30-50% loss
6. **Uncontrolled weeds** — 30-40% loss (biggest yield reducer)
7. **Pest/disease outbreaks** — 20-100% loss
8. **Soil degradation** — 10-20% gradual decline
9. **Post-harvest losses** — 15-25% of harvested produce
10. **No crop rotation** — 10-15% decline over years

## Seed Selection

### Quality Matters
- Use certified seeds from government/reputed sources
- Seed replacement ratio: Replace seeds every 3 years
- Hybrid seeds: Higher yield (20-30% more) but buy fresh each season
- Improved varieties: Developed by ICAR/SAUs for your region

### Seed Treatment
1. **Salt water test**: Dissolve 100g salt in 1 liter water. Dip seeds. Remove floaters (empty/light seeds). Wash remaining seeds.
2. **Fungicide treatment**: Carbendazim 2g or Thiram 3g per kg seed to prevent seed-borne diseases.
3. **Bio-agent treatment**: Trichoderma 5g/kg OR Pseudomonas 10g/kg for organic farming.
4. **Rhizobium inoculation**: For all legume crops — dramatically improves nitrogen fixation.

### Nursery Management
- Raised beds (10cm high) with FYM-enriched soil
- Proper spacing in nursery = stocky, healthy seedlings
- Harden seedlings before transplanting (reduce watering 2-3 days before)
- Optimal age for transplanting:
  - Rice: 20-25 day old seedlings
  - Tomato: 25-30 day old seedlings
  - Chilli/Brinjal: 35-40 day old seedlings

## Spacing Optimization

### Common Crops Spacing Guide
| Crop | Row x Plant (cm) | Plants per Acre |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Rice (transplanted) | 20 x 15 | ~133,000 |
| Rice (SRI method) | 25 x 25 | ~64,000 |
| Wheat | 20 x continuous | ~500 plants/sq.m |
| Tomato | 60 x 45 | ~14,800 |
| Chilli | 60 x 45 | ~14,800 |
| Brinjal | 75 x 60 | ~8,900 |
| Cotton | 120 x 60 | ~5,600 |
| Maize | 60 x 20 | ~33,300 |

### SRI Method (System of Rice Intensification)
- Single seedling transplanting (not 3-4)
- 25x25 cm spacing (wider than conventional)
- Transplant at 8-12 day age (younger)
- Alternate wetting and drying
- Mechanical weeder use
- **Result**: 30-50% more yield with 50% less water and 50% less seed

## Nutrient Management for Higher Yield

### Integrated Nutrient Management (INM)
- 50% nutrients from organic sources (FYM, compost)
- 50% from chemical fertilizers
- **This combination** gives higher yield than 100% chemical alone
- Add micronutrients based on soil test

### Key Nutrient Functions
| Nutrient | Deficiency Symptoms | Effect on Yield |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Nitrogen (N) | Yellowing of older leaves | Most dramatic yield loss |
| Phosphorus (P) | Purple stems, poor roots | Poor root and grain development |
| Potassium (K) | Brown leaf edges, weak stems | Lodging, poor grain fill |
| Zinc (Zn) | White bands in rice, stunting | 15-20% yield loss |
| Sulphur (S) | Yellowing of new leaves | Poor oil content in oilseeds |
| Boron (B) | Hollow stem in cauliflower | Flower/fruit drop |

### Foliar Nutrition for Quick Boost
- **2% Urea spray**: Quick nitrogen at critical stages (2g urea per 100ml)
- **DAP spray**: 2% solution at flowering
- **Micronutrient grades**: Commercial multi-micro sprays at flowering
- **Best time**: Early morning or late evening, avoid rain within 4 hours

## Weed Management Timeline

### Critical Period of Weed Competition (CPWC)
| Crop | CPWC (DAS/DAT) | Action Required By |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| Rice (transplanted) | 15-45 DAT | First weeding by 20 DAT |
| Wheat | 20-40 DAS | First weeding by 25 DAS |
| Maize | 15-45 DAS | First weeding by 20 DAS |
| Soybean | 20-40 DAS | Pre-emergent herbicide + 1 hand weeding |
| Cotton | 15-60 DAS | Needs 2-3 weedings |
| Tomato | 20-45 DAT | Keep weed-free transplanting to 45 days |

### Effective Weed Control Strategy
1. **Pre-emergent herbicide**: Apply within 3 days of sowing on moist soil
2. **One hand weeding**: At 25-30 DAS
3. **Second weeding (if needed)**: At 45-50 DAS
4. **After CPWC**: Crop canopy suppresses remaining weeds

## Crop Health Monitoring

### Weekly Field Walk Checklist
- [ ] Check 10 random plants for pest damage
- [ ] Look under leaves for eggs/larvae
- [ ] Check soil moisture (15cm depth)
- [ ] Monitor weed growth
- [ ] Look for nutrient deficiency symptoms
- [ ] Check irrigation system functioning
- [ ] Note any unusual plant growth
- [ ] Count beneficial insects (spiders, ladybugs, parasitic wasps)

### When to Act
| Observation | Action |
|------------|--------|
| 5% leaf damage | Monitor only |
| 10% leaf damage | Apply bio-control |
| 20%+ leaf damage | Chemical intervention |
| Sucking pests on 20% plants | Apply neem/chemical |
| Borer entry holes visible | Apply granular insecticide |

## Post-Harvest Management

### Reduce Losses (Current loss: 15-25% of production)
1. **Harvest at right moisture**: Grain crops at 14-18% moisture
2. **Sun drying**: Spread thin layer, turn 2-3 times daily, 2-3 days
3. **Clean storage**: Fumigate godown before storage
4. **Hermetic storage**: Air-tight storage bags (PICS bags) prevent insect damage
5. **Stack properly**: On dunnage (wooden pallets), away from walls
6. **Check regularly**: Monthly inspection for moisture, insects, rodents

### Value Addition
- Clean and grade produce before selling
- Process at home: Turmeric → powder, Tomato → paste, Chilli → powder
- Direct selling through FPO/Farmers Markets for 20-30% better price
- Government MSP procurement — check dates and centers

## Record Keeping

### What to Record
- Date of sowing and harvest
- Variety and seed source
- Fertilizer applied (type, quantity, date)
- Pesticide sprays (chemical, dose, date, target pest)
- Irrigation dates and method
- Weather events (rainfall, frost, hailstorm)
- Yield obtained and price received
- Expenses and income

### Why It Matters
- Compare performance year over year
- Identify what works and what doesn't
- Claim crop insurance (records serve as evidence)
- Plan next season better
- Track soil health changes over time
