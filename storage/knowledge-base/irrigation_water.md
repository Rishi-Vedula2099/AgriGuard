## Irrigation Methods

### Flood/Surface Irrigation
- **Most Common in India**: Used by 90%+ of farmers
- **Efficiency**: Only 30-40% efficient (60-70% water wasted)
- **Best For**: Rice (paddy requires standing water), large flat fields
- **Problems**: Waterlogging, salinity buildup, weed growth, nutrient leaching
- **Improvement Tips**:
  - Level fields properly using laser leveler
  - Use alternate wetting and drying (AWD) in rice
  - Border/furrow irrigation is better than wild flooding

### Drip Irrigation
- **Efficiency**: 85-95% (best water use efficiency)
- **Cost**: ₹30,000-60,000 per acre (government subsidy of 55-80% available)
- **Best For**: Vegetables (tomato, chili, brinjal), fruits (mango, pomegranate, citrus), sugarcane, cotton
- **Benefits**:
  - Saves 30-50% water vs flood irrigation
  - Can fertigate (apply fertilizer through drip = 30% fertilizer saving)
  - Reduces weed growth (only wets crop zone)
  - Reduces disease (leaves stay dry)
  - 20-40% higher yield
- **Components**: Main pipeline, sub-main, laterals, drippers (2-4 lph)
- **Maintenance**: Flush lines monthly. Check for clogging. Use filter. Acid treatment annually.
- **Government Subsidy**: Apply at your District Agriculture Office under PMKSY

### Sprinkler Irrigation
- **Efficiency**: 65-80%
- **Best For**: Wheat, groundnut, mustard, pulses, fodder crops
- **Types**: Portable (movable pipes), permanent (fixed), micro-sprinklers
- **Cost**: ₹20,000-40,000 per acre
- **Caution**: Not suitable during flowering (can damage pollen), not for crops prone to foliar diseases

### Micro-Sprinkler
- **Efficiency**: 80-90%
- **Best For**: Close-spaced crops, nurseries
- **Advantage**: Can cool plants in heat stress

## When to Irrigate — Signs of Water Stress

### Visual Signs
- **Wilting** during afternoon (temporary wilting is normal in hot weather; persistent wilting = needs water)
- **Leaf rolling** in rice and maize
- **Darker blue-green** leaf color (before wilting)
- **Slower growth** and smaller leaves

### Soil-Based Method
- Take a handful of soil from 15cm depth
- If it crumbles when squeezed → needs water
- If it holds shape and feels moist → adequate
- If water squeezes out → too wet (reduce irrigation)

### Simple Tools
- **Tensiometer**: Insert in root zone. Irrigate when reading reaches 30-60 cbar.
- **Feel and Appearance**: Check soil every 2-3 days

## Seasonal Water Management

### Kharif/Monsoon Season (June-October)
- **Reduce irrigation**: Rainfall usually sufficient
- **Focus on drainage**: Prevent waterlogging
- **Raised beds**: For vegetables to avoid root rot
- **Monitor**: Even in monsoon, dry spells of 7-10 days need supplemental irrigation
- **Rice AWD**: Alternate Wetting and Drying saves 20-30% water without yield loss

### Rabi/Winter Season (October-March)
- **Irrigate regularly**: No rainfall, critical period
- **First irrigation**: 21 days after sowing for wheat (Crown Root Initiation)
- **Light frequent irrigation**: Better than heavy infrequent
- **Frost protection**: Light irrigation evening before expected frost protects crop

### Zaid/Summer Season (March-June)
- **High water demand**: Peak temperatures, high evaporation
- **Mulching essential**: Reduces evaporation by 30-50%
- **Early morning irrigation**: 6-8 AM (less evaporation than midday)
- **Evening irrigation**: Acceptable but increases disease risk in some crops

## Water Conservation Techniques

### Mulching
- **Organic**: Rice straw (5-8cm), dried leaves, sugarcane trash
- **Plastic**: Black polythene mulch for vegetables
- **Benefits**: 30-50% less evaporation, weed control, soil temperature regulation
- **Cost**: Organic = free (from crop residues), Plastic = ₹5,000-8,000/acre

### Rainwater Harvesting
- **Farm pond**: Dig 3-5m deep pond to store monsoon runoff
- **Size**: 1/5th of farm area
- **Use**: Supplemental irrigation during dry spells
- **Government**: Subsidy available under PMKSY for farm ponds
- **Lining**: Silpaulin lining prevents seepage (₹300-500/sq meter)

### Land Leveling
- **Laser Leveling**: Government-subsidized service
- **Benefit**: Saves 20-25% irrigation water, uniform crop stand
- **Cost**: ₹3,000-5,000 per acre (one-time, lasts 3-5 years)

### Deficit Irrigation
- **Concept**: Apply less water than full requirement at non-critical growth stages
- **When**: Skip irrigation at vegetative stage (less sensitive), focus water on reproductive stage (most critical)
- **Water Saving**: 20-30% with minimal yield loss (5-10%)

## Common Irrigation Problems

### Waterlogging
- **Signs**: Yellowing lower leaves, root rot, foul smell from soil
- **Solution**: Improve drainage channels, raise beds, reduce irrigation
- **Long-term**: Install sub-surface drainage tiles

### Salinity
- **Signs**: White crust on soil surface, poor germination, stunted growth
- **Solution**: Leach with excess water, apply gypsum (2-5 tonnes/ha), grow salt-tolerant varieties
- **Crops**: Rice, barley, cotton are more salt-tolerant

### Iron Toxicity in Rice
- **Signs**: Bronze/orange discoloration of leaves
- **Solution**: Drain field, allow aerobic conditions, apply lime/potassium

## Tips for Small Farmers

1. **Cheapest improvement**: Level your field properly = instant 20% water saving
2. **Mulch with whatever you have**: Straw, dried leaves, even newspaper between rows
3. **Group irrigation**: Coordinate with neighbors for canal water turns
4. **Drip subsidy**: Check eligibility at Agriculture Office — can get 55-80% subsidy
5. **Moisture check**: Dig 15cm, feel soil — simplest and most reliable method
6. **Early morning watering**: Always better than evening (less disease)
7. **Avoid watering leaves**: Wet leaves = fungal diseases (especially tomato, potato)
