# Organic Farming & Pest Management — Knowledge Base
# Source: National Centre of Organic Farming (NCOF), ICAR

## Organic Pest Control Methods

### Neem-Based Solutions

#### Neem Oil Spray (Most Versatile)
- **Preparation**: 5ml neem oil + 1ml liquid soap in 1 liter water. Shake well.
- **Effective Against**: Aphids, whiteflies, mealybugs, caterpillars, leaf miners, mites
- **Frequency**: Spray every 7-10 days
- **Timing**: Spray in evening (neem oil degrades in sunlight)
- **Safety**: Completely safe for humans, beneficial insects (after drying)

#### Neem Kernel Extract (NSKE 5%)
- **Preparation**: Crush 50g neem seeds. Soak overnight in 1 liter water. Filter through cloth. Add 1ml liquid soap.
- **Effective Against**: Most chewing and sucking pests
- **Better than neem oil**: Higher azadirachtin content

#### Neem Cake Application
- **Rate**: 100-200 kg per acre mixed in soil
- **Benefits**: Kills soil nematodes, grubs, and root-feeding insects
- **Additional**: Acts as slow-release nitrogen source

### Botanical Pesticides

#### Chilli-Garlic Spray
- **Preparation**: Grind 100g hot chillies + 100g garlic in water. Soak overnight. Filter. Add to 10 liters water + 5ml soap.
- **Effective Against**: Aphids, mites, small caterpillars, whiteflies
- **Frequency**: Every 5-7 days during pest attack

#### Tobacco Extract (Nicotine)
- **Preparation**: Soak 500g tobacco dust in 4.5 liters water for 24 hours. Filter. Dilute to 10 liters.
- **Effective Against**: Aphids, thrips, caterpillars
- **Caution**: Toxic to honeybees — don't spray during flowering. Not truly organic.

#### Pyrethrum (from Chrysanthemum)
- **Use**: Natural insecticide from dried pyrethrum flowers
- **Effective Against**: Most flying and crawling insects
- **Note**: Also kills beneficial insects. Use only when necessary.

### Bio-Control Agents

#### Trichoderma viride / T. harzianum
- **What**: Beneficial fungus that attacks plant pathogens
- **Effective Against**: Root rot, damping off, wilt, collar rot
- **Application**:
  - Seed treatment: 5g per kg seed
  - Soil application: 2.5 kg mixed with 100 kg FYM per hectare
  - Seedling dip: 10g per liter water
- **Important**: Don't mix with chemical fungicides

#### Pseudomonas fluorescens
- **What**: Beneficial bacterium
- **Effective Against**: Bacterial diseases, root rot, wilt
- **Application**: Seed treatment 10g/kg seed, soil drench 5g/liter
- **Storage**: Keep refrigerated (2-8°C)

#### Beauveria bassiana
- **What**: Insect-pathogenic fungus (white muscardine)
- **Effective Against**: Borers, beetles, whiteflies, mealybugs
- **Application**: 5g per liter water spray or soil application
- **Advantage**: Self-propagating in insect populations

#### Metarhizium anisopliae
- **What**: Green muscardine fungus
- **Effective Against**: White grubs, root grubs, termites
- **Application**: Soil application 2.5 kg/ha with FYM

#### Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt)
- **What**: Bacterium producing insecticidal proteins
- **Effective Against**: All caterpillars/larvae (Helicoverpa, Spodoptera, DBM)
- **Application**: 1g per liter water. Spray when young larvae seen.
- **Advantage**: Very specific — safe for beneficial insects
- **Note**: Spray in evening for best results (UV degrades Bt)

#### Trichogramma (Egg Parasitoid)
- **What**: Tiny wasp that parasitizes pest eggs
- **Effective Against**: Bollworms, stem borers, fruit borers
- **Release**: 1-1.5 lakh eggs/ha (available as Tricho-cards from bio-control labs)
- **Timing**: Release at egg-laying stage of pest. Repeat 4-6 times.

### Physical/Mechanical Control

#### Yellow Sticky Traps
- **Effective Against**: Whiteflies, aphids, leaf miners, fungus gnats
- **Make at Home**: Yellow plastic sheet + castor oil/grease
- **Rate**: 10-15 traps per acre

#### Blue Sticky Traps
- **Effective Against**: Thrips specifically
- **Rate**: 10-15 traps per acre

#### Pheromone Traps
- **What**: Species-specific chemical lures that attract male moths
- **For**: Helicoverpa (cotton/chickpea), stem borer (rice), fruit fly (mango/guava)
- **Rate**: 5 traps per acre
- **Purpose**: Monitoring (decide spray timing) + mass trapping

#### Light Traps
- **What**: Bulb or LED above water tray
- **Attracts**: Moths, beetles, many flying pests
- **Use**: Monitor pest populations. Set up 3-4 per acre.

### Cultural Practices (Prevention)

1. **Deep ploughing in summer**: Exposes soil pests to heat and predators
2. **Crop rotation**: Breaks pest and disease cycles
3. **Removal of crop residue**: Eliminates pest hiding spots
4. **Border/trap crops**: Plant marigold around tomato (traps Helicoverpa)
5. **Companion planting**: Basil with tomato (repels whiteflies), onion with carrot (repels carrot fly)
6. **Bird perches**: Install T-shaped perches for predatory birds (10-15 per acre)
7. **Timely sowing**: Early or optimal sowing avoids peak pest periods
8. **Resistant varieties**: First line of defense

## Organic Disease Management

### Bordeaux Mixture (1%)
- **Preparation**: Dissolve 100g copper sulphate in 5 liters water (Solution A). Dissolve 100g lime in 5 liters water (Solution B). Slowly pour A into B while stirring. Total: 10 liters.
- **Effective Against**: Downy mildew, late blight, bacterial diseases
- **Caution**: Do not spray at temperatures >30°C or during flowering

### Panchagavya
- **Composition**: Cow dung, cow urine, cow milk, curd, ghee — fermented 15-20 days
- **Use**: 3% spray (30ml per liter). Growth promoter + mild disease suppressant.
- **Frequency**: Every 15 days during vegetative growth

### Jeevamrutham
- **Preparation**: 10 kg fresh cow dung + 10 liters cow urine + 2 kg jaggery + 2 kg pulse flour + handful of farm soil in 200 liters water. Ferment 4-7 days.
- **Application**: 200 liters per acre through irrigation or sprinkle
- **Benefit**: Excellent soil microbial activator

### Amritpani
- **Preparation**: 10 kg cow dung + 500g honey + 250g ghee. Mix well, dilute in 200 liters water.
- **Application**: Soil drench after sowing
- **Benefit**: Promotes root growth, microbial activity

## Weed Management (Organic)

### Prevention
- Dense planting/close spacing — suppress weeds by shade
- Cover crops — cowpea, horse gram as living mulch
- Stale seedbed technique — irrigate, let weeds germinate, then destroy before sowing

### Physical
- Hand weeding at 20 and 40 days after sowing
- Cycle weeder / cono-weeder in rice (saves labor)
- Mulching (plastic or organic)

### Bioherbicides
- Still limited commercial availability in India
- Vinegar spray (20% acetic acid) — kills small weeds on contact (not selective)

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM) Principles

1. **Prevention first**: Resistant varieties, crop rotation, clean planting material
2. **Observation**: Regular field scouting (2x per week). Learn pest identification.
3. **Threshold**: Don't spray unless pest population crosses Economic Threshold Level (ETL)
4. **Biological first**: Use bio-agents and botanicals before chemicals
5. **Chemical last resort**: If needed, use targeted chemicals (not broad-spectrum)
6. **Record keeping**: Note pest occurrence, treatments used, results — learn season by season
